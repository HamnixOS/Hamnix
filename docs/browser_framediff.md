## The metric

**SSIM is the primary parity signal** (structural similarity, 0..1, HIGHER ==
closer). Raw full-frame RMSE is a poor renderer metric: it area-scales (a big,
*correct* heading accumulates more absolute AA error than a tiny wrong render)
and it treats every subpixel-misregistered antialiased edge as a full error.
Historically raw RMSE *rose* when headings started rendering at true size —
punishing the fidelity gain. **SSIM** and the downsample+blur **brmse** (0..1,
LOWER = closer) both reward STRUCTURE (right boxes, right text at the right
place & size) and tolerate the AA/hinting/baseline differences a reader does
not care about. Raw RMSE stays as a continuity column only. See
`scripts/framediff_metric.py`. Pages are ranked worst-first by `1 - meanSSIM`.
